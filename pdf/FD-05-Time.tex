\begin{abstract}
We prove that time emerges from asymmetry in the genesis process. $K_4$ edges are bidirectional (symmetric), but the genesis drift from 3 to 4 distinctions is unidirectional (asymmetric). This fundamental asymmetry forces the Minkowski signature $\eta = \text{diag}(-1, +1, +1, +1)$: one negative (temporal) component and three positive (spatial) components. The temporal dimension is unique because the genesis process is irreversible. All results are machine-verified under \texttt{--safe --without-K} in Agda (7,938 lines). The mathematics is proven; physical interpretation remains a hypothesis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{The Problem of Time}

In standard physics, spacetime has signature $(-,+,+,+)$ (or $(+,-,-,-)$ in alternative conventions). This structure is \emph{assumed}, not derived. Questions arise:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Why is one dimension different (time)?
  \item Why negative signature for time?
  \item Why exactly one time dimension?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The Proposal}

We derive the Minkowski signature from asymmetry:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $K_4$ edges are \textbf{symmetric} (bidirectional): $(v_i, v_j) \leftrightarrow (v_j, v_i)$
  \item Genesis drift is \textbf{asymmetric} (unidirectional): $n=3 \to n=4$ (cannot reverse)
  \item Symmetric directions $\to$ positive signature (space)
  \item Asymmetric direction $\to$ negative signature (time)
  \item Result: $\eta = \text{diag}(-1, +1, +1, +1)$
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Prove $K_4$ edges are bidirectional (symmetric)

\begin{definition}[Reversibility]
\label{def:reversibility}
A structure is \textbf{reversible} if traversal in both directions is possible. It is \textbf{irreversible} (asymmetric) if traversal is unidirectional.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Why This Mapping?}

\textbf{Rationale:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Symmetric structures} (bidirectional) allow "return" to the same state: signature $+1$
  \item \textbf{Asymmetric structures} (unidirectional) enforce directionality, breaking symmetry: signature $-1$
  \item Time is the dimension in which \emph{irreversibility} manifests
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Physical analogy:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item In space: you can move left or right, up or down, forward or backward (reversible)
  \item In time: you can only move forward (asymmetric, irreversible)
\end{itemize}
